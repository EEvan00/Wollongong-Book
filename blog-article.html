Weekend Book Club Picks

Maya Chen 12 September 2024 6 minute read 3 comments

Every month, our weekend book club explores a new stack of stories that celebrate curious minds and bold storytelling. September's picks invited us to travel from coastal mysteries to intergenerational family dramas — and yes, there was plenty of lively debate (and almond croissants) along the way.

What We Read

We kicked things off with Black Night, our staff favourite thriller set right here on the Illawarra coastline. Members loved how detective Aria Byrne pieced together the lighthouse keeper's journal, and half the room is already campaigning for a sequel. We paired it with Saltwater Secrets, a slim novella that balances the darker themes with healing and community.

Discussion Highlights

  • The coded journal entries in Black Night and the way they echoed coastal folklore.
  • How environmental change shaped both novels — coastal erosion was a surprise talking point!
  • Favourite comfort reads to reset after an intense plot (lots of votes for cosy crime).
“Reading these back-to-back felt like watching the tide roll in and out — tension followed by calm. I stayed up way too late finishing both.” — Emily P.

We wrapped the session by swapping annotated copies and planning October's lineup, which will feature an atmospheric gothic novel and a hopeful speculative fiction pick. If you'd like to read along, you'll find the full list in-store and on our events board.
